Which Rules Control?

This Player Guide contains the event-specific rules for Ground Zero 2026. Standard pickleball rules apply to gameplay unless this guide or an official Ground Zero Event Bulletin states a specific modification.

Pre-Match Procedure

  1. Both teams report to the assigned court.
  2. The umpire verifies the players and match assignment.
  3. Coin Toss determines the first choice.
  4. The winner chooses either first service or starting end; the opponent receives the remaining option.
  5. Teams receive up to two minutes of official warm-up/dinking on the assigned court.
  6. The umpire signals the first serve and records the official start time.

No dedicated warm-up courts are guaranteed. Unauthorized casual play or warm-up on active tournament courts may be stopped immediately.

Match Administration

Transition Window

Up to five minutes for result submission, court clearance, player entry, Coin Toss, and official warm-up.

Medical Timeout

One medical assessment period of up to five minutes for an injury or medical incident, unless emergency care requires longer.

⚠️
Grace period starts from the official Qourts call—not from an outdated printed time. A team may not be forfeited before its court is available and Qourts has officially called the match.

Late Arrival and Forfeiture

A team is officially present only when both registered players are at the assigned court and ready to play.

  1. Qourts issues the official match call when the court is available.
  2. The five-minute grace period begins at that call.
  3. If the complete team is not ready when five minutes expires, Qourts records an 11–0 forfeit.
  4. A delay caused by Qourts, the venue, an ongoing official match, or a formally recognized emergency does not count against the team.
  5. Traffic, parking, meals, personal warm-up, failure to monitor announcements, and ordinary scheduling misunderstandings are not automatic exceptions.

Medical Timeout and Retirement

  • Play stops upon the umpire's official injury call.
  • The medical timeout begins at the official injury call and may last no more than five minutes.
  • A medic may stop a player from continuing when safety is at risk.
  • If the team cannot continue, the opponent receives 11 points and the retiring team retains its actual score at retirement.
  • Attempted misuse of a medical timeout to delay play may be treated as unsportsmanlike conduct.

Equipment and Match Ball

  • Only the tournament-issued official match ball may be used.
  • Personal balls may not replace the official ball.
  • Damaged balls may be replaced by the umpire.
  • Paddles must satisfy the equipment standard announced in the Event Bulletin and may be inspected when reasonably requested.
  • Refusal of a lawful equipment inspection may result in non-use of the equipment, match forfeit, or disciplinary action depending on the circumstances.

Paddle Modification and Permitted Accessories

Prohibited Modifications

Players shall use paddles that comply with the equipment standards announced in the Event Bulletin. Any modification intended to alter the paddle face, surface friction, spin potential, or other playing characteristics is strictly prohibited.

Prohibited modifications include, but are not limited to:

  • Applying grit, abrasive material, friction-enhancing substances, textured coatings, sprays, adhesives, or any similar material to the paddle face;
  • Sanding, resurfacing, scoring, etching, scraping, or otherwise changing the paddle face's original texture or roughness;
  • Drilling, shaving, cutting, delaminating, or structurally altering the paddle beyond the manufacturer's original specifications; and
  • Any alteration that creates an unfair competitive advantage or causes the paddle to fall outside the applicable equipment standard.

Permitted Accessories and Adjustments

The following are allowed, provided they do not alter the paddle's playing surface, conceal an unlawful modification, create a safety hazard, or otherwise violate the applicable equipment standard:

  • Edge guards and edge-guard tape;
  • Lead tape, tungsten tape, or comparable weight strips used for weight or balance adjustment;
  • Replacement grips, overgrips, grip sleeves, and grip build-up materials applied to the handle; and
  • Other protective, comfort, or balance accessories expressly approved by the Tournament Organizer.

Inspection and enforcement: Qourts or the Tournament Organizer may inspect any paddle before or during the tournament. A player must immediately stop using any paddle found, or reasonably believed, to violate this rule. Depending on the circumstances, refusal to cooperate or use of a prohibited paddle may result in paddle replacement, match forfeiture, disqualification, or other disciplinary action. The Tournament Organizer's decision is final and binding.

Team Timeout

Official Rule

  • Each team is entitled to one (1) timeout per match.
  • A timeout may only be requested by the serving team.
  • The request must be made while the ball is dead and before the umpire authorizes the next serve.
  • Each timeout shall not exceed one (1) minute.
  • Once play resumes, the timeout is considered used.
  • Unused timeouts do not carry over to another match.

Invalid Timeout Requests

A timeout request shall be denied when:

  • The requesting team is not the serving team.
  • The ball is in play.
  • The team has already used its timeout.
  • The request is made after the umpire has authorized the next serve.

Delay of Game

Players shall not intentionally delay play while waiting to regain the serve for the purpose of requesting a timeout. Repeated invalid timeout requests, stalling, or deliberate attempts to interrupt match flow may be penalized as Delay of Game.

Tiebreaking Order

If two or more teams finish pool play with the same win-loss record, the following criteria are applied in order. A later criterion is used only when the teams remain tied after the preceding criterion.

  1. Win-loss percentage. Wins divided by matches played.
  2. Overall point quotient. Total official points scored divided by total official points conceded.
  3. Head-to-head result. Used only when exactly two teams remain tied and those teams played each other.
  4. Tied-team mini-table. When three or more teams remain tied, only matches played among those tied teams are considered. Teams are ranked first by mini-table win-loss percentage, then by mini-table point quotient.
  5. Restart rule. If one or more teams separate from a multi-team tie, the process restarts at Step 1 for the teams that remain tied.
View the full tiebreak explanation and examples

How the process works

The criteria are applied one step at a time. As soon as a criterion clearly separates a team from the others, that team takes the corresponding position. The remaining tied teams are then evaluated again under the restart rule.

1. Win-Loss Percentage

This is calculated as Wins ÷ Matches Played. The team with the higher percentage ranks higher.

2. Overall Point Quotient

This is calculated as Total Official Points Scored ÷ Total Official Points Conceded. The higher quotient ranks higher because it reflects stronger overall scoring performance relative to points allowed.

3. Head-to-Head Result

This criterion applies only when exactly two teams remain tied and they played each other. The winner of their direct match ranks higher. It is not used to resolve a tie involving three or more teams.

4. Tied-Team Mini-Table

When three or more teams remain tied, a separate ranking is created using only the matches played among those tied teams. Results against teams outside the tie are excluded. The tied teams are ranked first by mini-table win-loss percentage and then, if still tied, by mini-table point quotient.

5. Restart Rule

When a team separates from a multi-team tie, the remaining tied teams return to Step 1. This prevents a criterion designed for a larger group from being carried forward after the composition of the tie has changed.

Example: Teams A, B, and C are tied. The mini-table places Team A above the others, while Teams B and C remain tied. Team A receives the higher position. Teams B and C then restart at Step 1; because only two teams remain tied, their head-to-head result may apply at Step 3.

Special Rule: Zero Points Conceded

A point quotient cannot be calculated when a team has conceded zero points because division by zero is undefined. Therefore:

  • A tied team that conceded zero points ranks above a tied team that conceded one or more points.
  • If multiple tied teams conceded zero points, the team with more total official points scored ranks higher.
  • If they are still equal, the process continues to the next applicable criterion.
Example: Team A scored 44 points and conceded 0. Team B scored 55 points and conceded 5. Team A ranks higher because it conceded no points, even though Team B has a finite quotient of 11.0.

Score Confirmation and Corrections

  • The umpire states the final score and both teams are given a reasonable opportunity to verify it.
  • The winning representative signs the Match Result Sheet. A signature confirms the recorded score but does not prevent correction of a proven encoding error.
  • Refusal to sign does not invalidate a result verified by the umpire and Qourts.
  • A court-level score objection must be raised before leaving the court whenever possible.
  • An encoding or standings error must be reported within ten minutes after the result or standings are publicly posted and before the affected playoff match begins, whichever occurs first.
  • Identity fraud, falsified records, or deliberate tampering may be reviewed at any time during the event.
  • Players may not alter, photograph for manipulation, carry away, or submit an official result sheet unless Qourts authorizes it.

Playoff Qualification and Seeding

Qourts validates all standings and announces playoff qualifiers and semifinal pairings only after the relevant pool results have been completed and encoded. Qualification is based on the published pool structure and ranking system—not popularity, registration order, or organizer preference.

Rules, timing, penalties, and ranking criteria should be applied consistently across similarly situated teams.

Players are entitled to public access to the controlling player guide, schedule information, standings, and applicable event bulletins.

Safety

Players may report unsafe conditions, request reasonable medical attention, and be removed from play when continuing would be unsafe.

Respectful Review

Accurate Records

Players may request correction of a demonstrable score-entry or identity error within the prescribed time.

Players have the right to compete without threats, discriminatory abuse, harassment, intimidation, or retaliation.

ℹ️

  • Do not transfer, share, sell, or allow another person to use your registration.

  • Report ready and properly equipped at the assigned court.
  • Know and follow the published match format, scoring, timeout, and side-switch rules.
  • Respect opponents, partners, officials, organizers, volunteers, spectators, and venue personnel.
  • Confirm results honestly and report errors immediately.
  • Follow safety, venue, equipment, and court-access instructions.
  • Control the conduct of personal coaches, companions, and supporters when reasonably directed.

Conduct & Discipline

Sportsmanship Code

Ground Zero 2026 is built on fair play, respect, integrity, and player safety. Every participant is expected to compete honestly, accept official match administration, and treat partners, opponents, officials, volunteers, venue staff, and spectators with dignity.

Respect the Game

Follow the rules, play honestly, and avoid conduct intended to gain an unfair advantage.

Respect People

Communicate professionally and avoid abuse, harassment, intimidation, or discriminatory remarks.

Respect Official Decisions

Raise concerns through the proper process without disrupting play or confronting officials aggressively.

  • Reckless paddle or ball throwing
  • Threatening gestures or conduct

  • Continuing behavior that creates an immediate safety risk
⚠️

Penalty Levels

Officials normally apply progressive discipline. However, Qourts and the Tournament Director may bypass lower levels when misconduct is severe, intentional, repeated, fraudulent, threatening, or dangerous.

Level 1

Verbal Reminder

Used for minor, first-time conduct issues that can be corrected immediately.

Level 2

Official Warning

Formally recorded for repeated minor conduct or a more serious first offense.

Level 3

Technical Penalty

One point is awarded to the opponent. If that point reaches the winning target, the match ends. A second technical penalty in the same match may result in forfeiture.

Level 4

Match Forfeit

The offending team loses the match when conduct materially affects match integrity, safety, or lawful continuation of play.

Level 5

Tournament Disqualification

The player or team is removed from the tournament for severe or repeated misconduct, fraud, aggression, harassment, or a major integrity breach.

Who May Impose a Penalty?

Assigned Umpire or Referee

May issue immediate reminders, warnings, and in-match sanctions within the scope of match administration.

Qourts — Official Match Control

May review incidents, confirm or escalate sanctions, administer forfeitures, document misconduct, and refer serious matters to the Tournament Director.

ZZT Tournament Director

May impose or confirm disqualification, venue removal, or other final event-level sanctions for severe or unresolved misconduct.

Appeal Scope

Factual or procedural errors may be reviewed. Ordinary judgment regarding the seriousness of observed conduct generally stands unless clearly unreasonable or inconsistent with the published rules.

Disciplinary Process

  • The official should identify the conduct and penalty level to the player when circumstances allow.
  • Qourts records Level 2 through Level 5 sanctions and the factual basis for the decision.
  • A player may request review for mistaken identity, factual error, procedural error, or a penalty outside the published range.
  • Immediate safety removal may occur before a full explanation. Review follows once the situation is controlled.
  • Sanctions apply only to the responsible player or team unless another person independently participated in the misconduct.
!
Separate offenses may carry separate penalties. A valid protest or complaint does not excuse abusive, threatening, or disruptive behavior during the process.

The schedule in this Player Guide reflects the planned tournament flow. Actual match times, court assignments, raffle draws, and awarding ceremonies may occur earlier or later depending on match duration, medical timeouts, court availability, and other operational adjustments made by Match Control.

All players must complete registration before their first match and should report to their assigned court area at least 30 minutes before their scheduled Round Robin match. Always follow announcements from Match Control, as they take precedence over the published schedule.

A Message of Gratitude to Our Sponsors and Partners

As we prepare for Ground Zero 2026, we would like to express our deepest gratitude to every sponsor, partner, and community supporter who chose to be part of this journey.

Your trust and generosity mean more than simply supporting a tournament. You are helping create an event where players can compete with passion, families and friends can come together, and new connections can be built through a shared love for pickleball. Your partnership is helping lay the foundation for an experience that celebrates sportsmanship, camaraderie, and the continued growth of our local pickleball community.

Every contribution—whether as a Platinum, Gold, Silver, Bronze Sponsor, or Community Supporter—plays an important role in bringing Ground Zero 2026 to life. Together, you are helping provide a well-organized, enjoyable, and memorable tournament experience for every participant, volunteer, official, and guest.

We are honored that you have placed your confidence in our vision. Your support inspires us to deliver an event that reflects the professionalism, integrity, and community spirit that all of us share.

As the tournament approaches, we look forward to celebrating not only great competition, but also the partnerships that make gatherings like this possible. We hope Ground Zero 2026 becomes the beginning of many more opportunities for our community to grow together, both on and off the court.

From the bottom of our hearts, thank you for believing in our vision and for standing with us as we bring Ground Zero 2026 to life.

With sincere appreciation, Zero Zero Two (ZZT) Organizer, Ground Zero 2026
